St Laurence Church

  • Nobody knows when or why St Laurence Church was built. It dates from around AD1000.
  • It was never the parish church, which may be buried beneath Holy Trinity church.
  • Possibly it was a shrine to King Edward the Martyr, murdered in A.D. 978.
  • A 15th century copy of a charter states that Bradford was to be used to shelter Edwardâ€™s relics against Viking raids.
  • Further Information
  • The church is a Grade 1 listed building, described as a building of exceptional archaeological value.
  • It is one of only a few complete Saxon churches in Great Britain, and as such is extremely rare.
  • Most other Saxon churches have been substantially altered at a later date or survive as portions embedded in later structures.
  • The church is managed by Trustees
A significant event was the drawing of the elevations by W C Lukis in 1858. Canon Jones who discovered the building commented: "The design and nature of the building entirely escaped the notice of archaeologists". These drawings are used here to produce a 3D model, roughly representing how the new discovery was perceived.
